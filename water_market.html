Revenue per ML ($/ML) distributions across all 560 simulated farmers, by crop type. Dark bars show seasonal farm-gate revenue. Lighter extensions show the switching-cost premium — the annualised asset-destruction risk that raises perennial farmers' effective willingness-to-sell well above their seasonal revenue.

Why perennials are reluctant sellers. Vines and almond trees take 4–7 years to re-establish at $15,000–30,000/HA. Dairy herds cannot be rebuilt overnight. Even if the spot price exceeds seasonal revenue per ML, selling water risks destroying the permanent asset — so the effective price at which a perennial farmer will sell is much higher than the revenue number alone suggests.

Estimated switching-cost premiums above seasonal revenue: Dairy +$150–250/ML · Grapes +$200–350/ML · Citrus +$250–400/ML · Almonds +$300–500/ML. Rice and cotton have no switching cost.
